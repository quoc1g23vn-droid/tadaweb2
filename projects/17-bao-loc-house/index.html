Bao Loc House

2024

Location: Bao Loc, Lam Dong

Bao Loc House is a small rural dwelling located on the outskirts of Bao Loc, where the landscape still retains the character of an agricultural countryside largely untouched by rapid urbanization. The site gently slopes toward a nearby river, offering ideal natural conditions for creating a living environment that is closely integrated with its surroundings.

The house was designed for a young couple seeking to combine everyday living with remote working. With a floor area of approximately 90 square meters, the project is based on a simple structural system and constructed using locally available materials, while providing living spaces that are compact, sufficient, and adaptable. Beyond meeting the family's essential daily needs, the house also serves as an exploration of a contemporary and sustainable model for rural living.

The project aspires to achieve a degree of self-sufficiency by generating part of its own energy and food, thereby reducing dependence on external resources. More than simply providing shelter from the elements, the house is conceived as a place that nurtures well-being, enabling its occupants to maintain a healthy balance between work and leisure while fostering a close relationship with nature.

In designing Bao Loc House, our goal was to reinterpret the rural home for a new era—one that carefully balances contemporary comfort with the intrinsic values of its setting. The project is guided by the following key design strategies:

The house is designed to minimize its environmental footprint through the integration of solar energy and rainwater harvesting systems. Generous areas are also allocated for vegetable gardens and fruit trees, allowing the family to partially produce its own food while meeting part of its energy needs in a sustainable manner.

The living and working environments are thoughtfully integrated to support remote work while maintaining a healthy balance between professional and personal life. The spatial organization allows the homeowners to work efficiently without disrupting the rhythm of everyday domestic activities.

The living spaces are conceived as multi-functional environments that can easily adapt to different uses, including working, relaxing, and socializing. This flexibility enables the house to respond to the changing needs of its occupants over time. Several architectural details also provide users with multiple ways of inhabiting and interacting with the spaces.

The project employs simple, locally sourced materials to reduce transportation costs while responding appropriately to the local climate and construction practices. Bao Loc House addresses many of the limitations of conventional self-built rural housing and proposes a new generation of rural homes—contemporary in performance while remaining deeply rooted in the spirit of place.

More than a place to live and work, the house is envisioned as an environment that nurtures emotional well-being. Its open spatial arrangement and strong connection to nature create a calm and restorative atmosphere. This quality resonates with the aspirations of a modern, active young couple who have chosen the countryside as a place to build their life.
